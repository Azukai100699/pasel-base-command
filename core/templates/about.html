OPERATIONAL ROADMAP

Philosophy & Objectives

"To engineer a self-sustaining future for Liberia by introducing practical scientific training, localizing manufacturing protocols, and deploying scalable structural solutions across all fifteen counties."

The 4 Engineering Pillars
Pillar 01
SCOPE: NATIONWIDE (15 COUNTIES)

Agricultural Mechanization

Scaling national food security networks by introducing mechanized soil management and advanced winch plowing technologies tailored to regional terrains. This infrastructure empowers local farming cooperatives to maximize seasonal yields.

Pillar 02
SCOPE: TARGETED DEPLOYMENT (MARGIBI ANCHOR)

Structural & Rammed Earth Systems

Advancing materials science research and automated rammed earth construction prototyping. This initiative optimizes sustainable, climate-resilient building methodologies, establishing its operational baseline in Margibi County before deploying to auxiliary sectors.

Pillar 03
SCOPE: NATIONWIDE (15 COUNTIES)

Aviation & Aerospace Intelligence

Cultivating a high-tech domestic manufacturing mindset across Liberia. PASEL partners directly with local high schools, training students in custom uncrewed aerial vehicle (UAV) fabrication, flight dynamics, and payload assembly to secure our country's future in aerospace technology.

Pillar 04
SCOPE: CENTRAL INTEGRATION

Industrial Technology

Accelerating rapid prototyping, high-precision manufacturing, and custom components design through the mastery of CNC machining and digital automated systems to support all heavy-industry deployments.

PASEL KEY INITIATIVE

THE NATIONAL DRONE TOURNAMENT

A nationwide competitive bracket designed to generate public enthusiasm, catalyze scientific curiosity, and showcase the tactical drone capabilities built by our high school training teams across the country. This tournament serves as the public validation arena for domestic aviation advancement.
